My 2 Cents Banking/Investment

About

As a team of about 20 members, we added features and functionalities to this existing banking application. We added functionalities that allow users to help budget their money and invest in crypto/stocks. The project is written in C# for the backend web api and the front end uses Angular. The original application allows users to login or register if they do not have an account. It then lets the access their checking and savings accounts. Track their flow of money to/from the accounts and to transfer funds between the accounts. They also have a profile page with some other ther information needed for the account. Below I go into more details about these features the team I was part of added to this app.

Technologies

This is a list of technologies used in our app. The main ones inclue ASP.NET Identity for Authentication, Microsoft Azure for our SQL DB + Web API, and Angular for the front end using Node JS and Bootstrap. The Back end also had unit testing with MOQ and the front end used Jasmine and Karma for testing.

Login

The login uses the username/email of the user to log them into the system. We used ASP.NET Core Identity to accomplish this. That way the data is encrypted through Identity and we are able to have a token for the session. As part of our project, we added Two Factor Authentication to the app. Since it is a banking app, adding more security is crucial in order to prevent break-ins. You get an email after clicking login which prompts you to click a link to verify the two-factor. Once done, you can access the pages in our app.

Register

If you do not have an account, you can register for one on this page. It opens a card to input your information. Once done, it will prompt you to setup Two Factor Authentication. Once that is done, you will be taken to the profile page in order to fill out the rest of the needed account information. After this, you can start using the app!

Stock/Crypto Investing

The app allows users to invest in stocks and crypto. The stocks uses the Yahoo Finance data for stock prices and displays real-time data onto the chart. Users can buy in either USD or in Shares of that company. There are checks that do not allow you to buy/sell more than you can based on your funds/owned shares. Most of this info goes the same for crypto, but instead of using Yahoo Finance data, the data is grabbed from Coin Gecko.

Investment Portfolio

There is a section that allows users to see their investments split up between stocks and crypto. They are also able to see their total investment. They are able to see the lists of all the stocks and crypto they own, the date they bought it, amount invested (USD), shares owned, price per share (USD), gain/loss, and the USD value of that gain/loss.

Investment Diversity

This is a pie chart that shows the user a visual representation of where their money is on the app. They are able to see the total amount they have in Checking, Savings, Crypto, and Stocks throughtout all their accounts and different company shares/crypto coins they may own. They are also able to interact with the chart's key at the top. When they click on one of the elements in the key, it will hide/show this from the chart. This allows them to see their investments on a smaller scale. Finally, the user can hover over the information â“˜ to read about how to use the chart.

Budget Calculator

This part of the app allows users to calculate their budget easily. All the user needs to do is input their income and expenses and then the app will calculate their ideal budget. It does this by using the 50 - 30 - 20 Rule. This rule basically says that you should divide up your after-tax income and allocate it to spend: 50% on needs, 30% on wants, and 20% into savings. The app will take away from users wants if their needs category is larger than 50%. It also takes a few other things into consideration when calculating. Finally, This information is shows and explained to the user at each step to give them a detailed explaination. They also recieve a graph for a visual representation.
